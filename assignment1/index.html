Luni Website Research

Interactive Media Assignment 1

by Tingjia

Luni App

Q1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

  • The home page design of Luni's website is simple but attractive. The first thing I noticed about this site was the use of 3D modeling, which is innovative and enhances the user's immersion experience. The home page is divided into four main interactive sections: "Studio", "Omada", "Career" and "Luni Life", each with bright colors and dynamic elements, and users can interact with these sections by clicking on them. The overall design is clear and fun, making you want to explore more.

Q2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

In the process of experiencing Luni website, I first had a preliminary understanding of the overall structure and various sections of the website through the 3D model of the home page.

  • I clicked on the "Omada" page and got an in-depth look at their main games, including their features, positioning, and user value.
  • I went into the "Studio" section to learn about the other products they were working on and to see the creative ideas of the team behind them.
  • On the "Career" page, I checked the job postings and felt that they attach importance to creativity and works.
  • Finally, I checked the "Luni Life" section to learn more about the company's internal culture, working environment and team atmosphere, which gave me a clearer sense of Luni's overall brand image.
  • After all the browsing, I went back to the home page.

Q4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

  • In the whole experience, my most common action is to slide the page to the top after browsing part of the content, click the "cross" button in the upper right corner to exit the current module and return to the initial state of the page. Then, I will continue the experience by clicking on the navigation bar in the lower right corner and selecting other sections.

Q5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

  • I think the main purpose of this website is to showcase the Luni brand and let more users know about the brand and the team. Attract the attention of more potential users by providing 3D models and interesting interaction design. Make the overall user experience more comfortable and smooth.

Q6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

  • The main goal of the Luni website is to show and promote the product, so that more people can learn about the brand and the team. Through 3D modeling and interesting interactive experience, it attracts users' attention and has a closer exploration.
  • The website has a simple navigation, which is fixed in the lower right corner of the page, to help users quickly switch between different sections and understand the core content of the company. At the same time, the whole layout is simple and orderly. Users only need to click to enter the module they are interested in, browse the detailed information, and then click "exit" to return to the initial interface, and switch to other plates freely. This intuitive interaction enables users to have a more complete and clear understanding of Luni in a relatively short time.

Q7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

  • After the experience, I think this site is more inclined to short-term participation design. Its main function is to intuitively introduce and display the brand, products and team, and play a role in publicity and promotion. The website quickly conveys the core information of Luni through beautiful visual effects and simple structure, so it is more suitable for users to get to know in a short time. As a whole, it's not designed to keep users scrolling through the content over and over again, but rather to attract the attention of potential users or job seekers through one-off impressions.

Q8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

  • Luni website is similar to the tourist mode that does not require login, so that users can enter the website and learn about it in the shortest time, improving the efficiency of the first visit, and there is no need to waste time on registration and login, so as to avoid losing some potential users. This is also in line with the site's goal to quickly showcase brands and products, ensuring that users can get key information in the shortest possible time. I think some of the more long-term use and multiple visits will be on their product games.

Q9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

  • Luni as a product display and promotion website, the style is closer to the product manual, in the most concise and clear way to introduce users to their product team and concept, users can quickly get the content they need.
  • At the same time, it is also similar to the form of "scenic guide manual" - the home page is like a map, which presents the main sections at a glance, and users can independently plan the browsing order according to their personal interests, and freely switch modules to explore. This free browsing makes the entire interactive experience easier and more intuitive.

Q10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

  • I think the layout of the Luni website is somewhat similar to that of product manuals and guidebooks, which guide users to explore in an orderly, modular manner, much like a person reading a product manual or viewing a map of an attraction would look at the highlights item by item.
  • When users enter the site, they will see a 3D layout similar to the "guide map", with four main sections (Studio, Omada, Career, Luni Life). This design encourages the user to click on the section of interest, view the content in turn, and quickly get to the key information.

Q11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

  • Luni website by combining the visual style of product brochures and 3D interactive models, so that users can create a new and bright feeling when entering the website. This design allows users to feel a relaxed atmosphere while browsing. Different from boring traditional corporate websites, Luni's interaction design is more creative and dynamic, guiding users to participate in the experience with a curious mind. Users do not need to seriously "learn" the content of the site, but like leafing through an interactive manual, happily learn about brands and products.

Q12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

  • For me, the most frustrating part of the interactive experience is that when visiting the homepage for the first time, it is difficult to understand exactly what the core purpose of the website is trying to convey. The front page is dominated by 3D models, which, while innovative, does not clearly convey that this is a website about product and brand display. When first browsing, I even mistook it for a home or lifestyle related platform. It would be much better if the sections were more clearly differentiated or if there was a short text introduction.
  • In addition, during the actual browsing process, I also encountered some buttons could not be clicked, which made the website lack of interactive content, but also let me feel some regret.

Q13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

  • For me, the most satisfying part of the interactive experience was the 3D modeling used on the site, the way it interacted, and the overall color mix. These elements were the first highlights I was attracted to when I entered the site. Through the form of dioramas and dynamic interaction, Luni makes product promotion more vivid and not boring, adding a lot of surprises to the entire browsing process. Users acquire information while maintaining an interest in continuous exploration.
  • In addition, the color of the website is fresh and layered, and the overall visual experience is relaxed, professional and modern.
  • At the same time, the navigation is also very clear, users can quickly locate the section of interest, smoothly switch between the modules to browse.
